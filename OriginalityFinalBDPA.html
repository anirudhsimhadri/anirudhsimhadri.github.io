The term A.I, or Artificial intelligence gets used by many people, but usually not with the correct definition.


The purest definition of A.I. was first introduced into the world by someone named John McCarthy in 1955 at the Dartmount Conference.


McCarthy says, "Every aspect of learning or any other feature of intelligence can in principle be so precisely described that a machine can be made to simulate it. An attempt will be made to fin how to make machines use language, form abstractions and concepts, solve kinds of problems now reserved for humans, and improve themselves."


In small, A.I. is a machiine with the ability to solve problems that are normally solved by humans with the natural intelligence of mankind intelligence.


The 1955 proposal defines 7 areas of A.I. The original seven are Simulating Higher Functions of the Human Brain, Programming a computer to use general lanuage, Arranging hypothetical neurons in a manner so that they can form concepts, A way to deterimine and measure problem complexity, self improvement, abstraction: defined as the quality of dealing with ideas rather than events, Randomness and creativity.


All of these proposals that define the 7 areas of A.I. lead to the world that it is now. Although that might not be the good and moral world that you might think either.
